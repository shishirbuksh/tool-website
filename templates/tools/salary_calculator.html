Understand Your Paycheck with Our Free Salary Calculator

Gross vs Net Pay

Gross Pay is the total money you make before any taxes or deductions are taken out. Net Pay (Take-Home Pay) is the actual money you get in your bank account to spend or save.

What are Deductions?

Deductions are amounts taken out of your gross pay. This usually includes income taxes, provident fund (PF), health insurance, and other benefits. Our tool lets you add these easily as a percentage or a fixed amount.

Hourly & Overtime

If you are paid by the hour, you can easily switch the calculator to "Per Hour" mode. You can also turn on the "Overtime" feature to see exactly how much extra money you make when working longer hours.

Visual Breakdown

It is easier to plan your budget when you know exactly where your money is going. Our advanced doughnut chart splits your salary into Take-Home Pay, Income Tax, Retirement, and Insurance so you can see the big picture.

Navigating the Indian Salary Calculator (New vs Old Tax Regime)

Calculating your exact take-home pay in India can be complex due to the changing tax laws, notably the choice between the New Tax Regime and the Old Tax Regime. Under the Old Regime, you can claim various exemptions such as House Rent Allowance (HRA), Leave Travel Allowance (LTA), and Section 80C deductions. The New Regime offers lower tax rates but removes most of these exemptions, though the Standard Deduction of ₹50,000 has been introduced for salaried employees.
