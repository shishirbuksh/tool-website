How to Avoid Crypto Tax Legally & Save Money

Understanding the tax guidelines allows you to use legal strategies to lower your tax liability. One of the most effective methods is **crypto tax loss harvesting**, which means selling off specific coins that are currently worth less than what you paid for them. These realized paper losses can offset your total capital gains for the tax year. In jurisdictions like the United States, you can offset your capital gains completely, and write off up to $3,000 of ordinary income each year, rolling over any extra losses to future tax cycles. Short-Term vs. Long-Term Capital Gains Crypto

Holding an asset for more than 365 days qualifies you for long-term capital gains tax brackets. These brackets offer preferential rates of 0%, 15%, or 20% in the US, compared to short-term gains which are taxed at standard ordinary income rates. Our calculator automatically counts the holding period in days for every matched lot to apply the correct progressive tax rates.

Understanding the Wash Sale Rule in Crypto Taxes

The wash sale rule prevents you from claiming a capital loss on an asset if you buy back a "substantially identical" asset within 30 days before or after the sale. Currently, IRS wash sale rules apply to stocks and securities, but the treatment of crypto continues to evolve. Staying compliant is crucial to avoid penalty interest and optimize offsets legally.
