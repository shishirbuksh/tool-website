Comprehensive Guide to the Crypto Fear & Greed Index Tracker

The Crypto Fear and Greed Index Tracker is an essential tool for reading market sentiment. Extreme fear can be a buying opportunity, while extreme greed indicates the market may be due for a correction.

How to Use the Tool

  1. Check the Gauge: Look at the main dial. A score near 0 means 'Extreme Fear', while a score near 100 means 'Extreme Greed'.
  2. Review Historical Data: Scroll down to see the timeline chart comparing today's sentiment against yesterday, last week, and last month.
  3. Cross-Reference Price: Compare the current index score with Bitcoin's price movements to spot divergence patterns.
  4. Set Alerts: Check back daily, as the index updates every 24 hours based on new market volatility and social volume.

How We Calculate the Data

Volatility Weighting (25%)

The algorithm measures current volatility and max drawdowns of Bitcoin and compares it with the average values of the last 30 and 90 days.

Market Momentum/Volume (25%)

High buying volume in a positive market indicates greedy behavior, while high selling volume indicates fear.

Social Media Trends (15%)

The tool analyzes Twitter sentiment. High interaction rates on specific crypto hashtags result in a greedier score.

Dominance & Trends (35%)

Bitcoin dominance and Google Trends data are factored in. When BTC dominance shrinks, it often signals altcoin greed.
