Mastering Advanced Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

How to calculate customer acquisition cost formula

The core customer acquisition cost formula divides Total Sales & Marketing Expenses by New Customers. However, standard calculators fail to separate Organic vs. Paid traffic. Our free blended cac vs paid cac calculator isolates these metrics. Blended CAC shows overall efficiency, while Paid CAC reveals the harsh reality of how expensive your ads actually are.

Using a Cost Per Lead Calculator

CAC is the end result, but CPL (Cost Per Lead) is the start. By entering your Total Leads into our cost per lead calculator, you can instantly find your funnel conversion rate. If your CPL is low but your CAC is high, your sales team (or landing page) is failing to convert leads into paying customers.

The Customer Retention Cost Formula

Most founders ignore the customer retention cost formula (CRC). Getting a customer is only half the battle; supporting them costs money too. By factoring in Customer Success salaries, our engine calculates your Net LTV. This guarantees your Payback Period is 100% accurate and prevents you from running out of cash.

What is a good LTV to CAC ratio for SaaS?

So, what is a good LTV to CAC ratio for SaaS? Investors look for a 3:1 ratio based on Net LTV. This means your customer generates three times more profit than they cost to acquire and support. If your ratio is lower, you must learn how to reduce customer acquisition cost in ecommerce or SaaS by optimizing your ad channels.
